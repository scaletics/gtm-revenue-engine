# Partner Qualification Scorecard

**Owner:** BD Lead
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Status:** Active
**Review Cadence:** Quarterly
**Audience:** Business Development, Partnerships, Revenue Leadership
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Purpose

Use this scorecard to determine whether a potential partner is worth pursuing.

The goal is to avoid low-yield partnerships that consume time without creating pipeline, credibility, access, or strategic leverage.

## Partner Fit Categories

Score each category from 1 to 5.

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| ICP access | 20% |
| Revenue potential | 20% |
| Strategic alignment | 15% |
| Ease of activation | 15% |
| Credibility value | 10% |
| Co-marketing potential | 10% |
| Operational complexity | 10% |

## Scoring Guidance

- 5: Exceptional fit
- 4: Strong fit
- 3: Possible fit
- 2: Weak fit
- 1: Do not pursue

## Recommendation Bands

| Score | Recommendation |
|---:|---|
| 4.25-5.00 | Strategic partner |
| 3.50-4.24 | Pursue |
| 2.75-3.49 | Test selectively |
| Below 2.75 | Do not pursue |

## Evaluation Questions

### ICP Access

- Does this partner already serve our target buyers?
- Can they introduce us to decision-makers?
- Do they have active trust with the buyer segment?

### Revenue Potential

- Can the partner influence qualified pipeline?
- Can the partner source recurring revenue opportunities?
- Is there a realistic commercial model?

### Strategic Alignment

- Does the partner strengthen our category position?
- Does the partner improve market access?
- Does the partner support enterprise value creation?

### Ease of Activation

- Can we launch a joint motion in 30 to 60 days?
- Are the required stakeholders accessible?
- Is there a clear first campaign or customer list?

### Operational Complexity

- Will this require heavy enablement?
- Will this require legal or procurement delays?
- Does the partner need custom support before producing value?
