## Purpose

Use this guide to determine whether an opportunity, campaign, client engagement, or GTM asset should follow the SMB SaaS, mid-market SaaS, or MSP path.

Correct segment routing improves messaging, sales process, pricing logic, qualification, and proposal structure.

---

## Routing Rule

Route by buying motion and business model, not just company size.

A small company with enterprise buyers may need a mid-market SaaS motion. A larger MSP with owner-led decisions may still need an MSP-specific recurring revenue motion.

---

## Segment Definitions

### SMB SaaS

Use when the buyer has:

- Simple buying process
- Founder-led or first-sales-hire motion
- Short sales cycle
- Limited stakeholders
- Lower ACV
- Urgency around pipeline, conversion, activation, or churn

Typical buyer:

- Founder
- CEO
- Head of Growth
- First Sales Hire

### Mid-Market SaaS

Use when the buyer has:

- Multiple stakeholders
- 3 to 6 month sales cycle
- ROI scrutiny
- Security or procurement review
- Department-level budget
- Need for business-case support

Typical buyer:

- CRO
- VP Sales
- VP Marketing
- RevOps Leader
- CEO
- Business Unit Leader

### MSP / Managed Services

Use when the buyer sells or manages:

- Recurring IT services
- Cybersecurity services
- Cloud services
- Microsoft services
- Hosting or infrastructure
- Compliance or managed operations

Typical buyer:

- Owner
- CEO
- GM
- VP Sales
- Service Delivery Leader
- Vendor / Channel Leader

---

## Routing Questions

Ask these questions before selecting a segment:

1. What does the company sell?
2. Who owns the buying decision?
3. How many stakeholders are involved?
4. Is the revenue model SaaS, services, managed services, or hybrid?
5. Is the main problem acquisition, conversion, sales process, packaging, expansion, or recurring revenue?
6. Is the buying motion simple, consultative, or channel-led?
7. Does the buyer need a proposal, business case, mutual action plan, or simple checkout path?

---

## Fast Routing Matrix

| Signal | Segment |
|---|---|
| Founder is primary seller | SMB SaaS |
| Buyer has formal procurement | Mid-Market SaaS |
| Services need to become recurring revenue | MSP |
| Sales cycle under 45 days | SMB SaaS |
| Sales cycle over 90 days | Mid-Market SaaS |
| Buyer is MSP owner | MSP |
| Business case required | Mid-Market SaaS |
| Offer packaging is unclear | MSP or SMB SaaS |
| Main issue is attach rate | MSP |
| Main issue is consensus | Mid-Market SaaS |

---

## Default Decision

When uncertain:

- Choose SMB SaaS if the buyer is founder-led and speed matters.
- Choose mid-market SaaS if there are multiple decision stakeholders.
- Choose MSP if the buyer monetizes recurring technology services.
