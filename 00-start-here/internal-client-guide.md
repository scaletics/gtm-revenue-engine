# Internal and Client Guide

**Owner:** Operations
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Review Cadence:** Quarterly
**Status:** Active
**Audience:** Sales, BD, Marketing, Client Delivery
**Client Reuse:** Internal Only

## Purpose

This guide explains how to separate internal GTM operating content from client-ready templates.

The goal is to protect internal strategy while making the repository easy to reuse for client engagements.

## Content Categories

### Internal Only

Use this label for content that includes proprietary strategy, internal pricing, private customer examples, CRM notes, forecast assumptions, or operating decisions.

Do not share Internal Only documents with clients.

### Client Safe

Use this label for content that can be shared with clients without editing.

Client Safe content should not include internal pricing, private customer names, CRM notes, or confidential strategy.

### Client Customizable

Use this label for templates designed to be adapted for a specific client.

Before sharing, replace internal examples with client-specific details.

## Before Sharing With a Client

Confirm:

- Internal company references are removed.
- Internal pricing is removed or approved.
- Private customer names are removed.
- CRM screenshots or notes are removed.
- The client ICP replaces the internal ICP.
- The client offer replaces the internal offer.
- The client sales motion replaces the internal sales motion.
- The file is marked Client Safe or Client Customizable.

## Internal Use Rules

Internal documents may include:

- Strategic rationale
- Decision history
- Internal examples
- Pricing assumptions
- Pipeline lessons
- Sales call learnings
- Competitive observations

Keep these details out of client-ready files unless explicitly approved.

## Client Customization Process

1. Copy the relevant internal asset.
2. Remove internal-only content.
3. Replace company, ICP, offer, pricing, and examples.
4. Align language to the client segment.
5. Update metadata.
6. Review for confidentiality.
7. Publish into the client workspace.

## Segment Adjustments

### SMB SaaS

Use simple language, short checklists, and fast execution steps. Avoid enterprise-heavy terminology.

### Mid-Market SaaS

Include stakeholder alignment, business case, procurement, risk, and implementation considerations.

### MSP

Emphasize recurring revenue, attach rate, service packaging, service margin, and account expansion.
