**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Purpose

This document defines the standard sales process used across SMB SaaS, mid-market SaaS, and MSP opportunities.

The goal is to create one repeatable sales motion with segment-specific adjustments, not separate disconnected sales processes.

## Sales Process Overview

| Stage | Purpose | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Target Identified | Confirm account fit | Account matches segment and priority criteria |
| 2. Qualified Lead | Confirm problem and next step | Qualification checklist completed |
| 3. Discovery Completed | Understand pain and business impact | Discovery notes captured |
| 4. Solution Aligned | Map solution to priorities | Buyer confirms relevance |
| 5. Business Case Built | Quantify value and urgency | Decision case documented |
| 6. Proposal Sent | Present scope and investment | Proposal delivered to stakeholders |
| 7. Negotiation | Resolve commercial concerns | Verbal approval or redlines received |
| 8. Closed Won / Lost | Capture final decision | Outcome and reason recorded |
| 9. Handoff / Expansion | Transition and identify growth path | Handoff completed |

## Segment-Level Sales Motion

### SMB SaaS

Primary motion: founder-led or inside sales.

Typical sales cycle: 7 to 45 days.

Main risk: no urgency or weak budget.

Best approach:

- Keep process simple.
- Focus on conversion, pipeline, and speed to revenue.
- Avoid overbuilding business-case materials.
- Use short pilots or focused sprints.

### Mid-Market SaaS

Primary motion: consultative sales.

Typical sales cycle: 3 to 6 months.

Main risk: consensus failure.

Best approach:

- Map stakeholders early.
- Build business case.
- Use mutual action plan.
- Address procurement, security, and implementation risk.

### MSP / Managed Services

Primary motion: recurring revenue and business outcome sale.

Typical sales cycle: 30 to 120 days.

Main risk: service commoditization.

Best approach:

- Focus on recurring revenue, attach rate, margin, and differentiation.
- Tie GTM work to service packaging.
- Use existing customer expansion as the first revenue path.
- Avoid positioning as another tool or generic consulting project.

## Required CRM Fields

Every opportunity must include:

- Segment
- ICP fit
- Primary pain
- Business impact
- Economic buyer
- Decision process
- Decision date
- Budget status
- Competitors
- Next step
- Close plan
- Loss reason, if applicable

## Stage Details

### 1. Target Identified

A target is identified when the company appears to match one of the approved ICPs.

Required signals:

- Industry fit
- Company size fit
- Business model fit
- Clear potential pain
- Relevant buyer persona identified
- Reasonable ability to pay

Exit criteria:

Move to Qualified Lead only when there is enough evidence to justify outreach or active pursuit.

### 2. Qualified Lead

A qualified lead has a clear problem, reachable buyer, potential commercial value, and a logical next step.

Use this qualification standard:

```text
Problem
Impact
Urgency
Fit
Authority
Next Step
```

Minimum qualification standard:

- The problem is real.
- The problem has business impact.
- The company fits one of the target segments.
- There is a plausible buyer.
- There is a clear next action.

### 3. Discovery Completed

Discovery is complete when the seller understands:

- Current state
- Desired future state
- Pain
- Business impact
- Buying process
- Decision criteria
- Stakeholders
- Budget posture
- Timeline
- Competing priorities

Required discovery output:

```text
Customer problem:
Business impact:
Current workaround:
Decision owner:
Decision process:
Success definition:
Risks:
Next step:
```

### 4. Solution Aligned

The solution is aligned when the buyer can clearly explain why the proposed solution matters.

Exit criteria:

- Buyer confirms the problem is worth solving.
- Buyer agrees the proposed solution is relevant.
- Buyer understands the expected outcome.
- Buyer agrees on the next step.

### 5. Business Case Built

A business case is required for:

- Mid-market SaaS deals
- MSP recurring revenue engagements
- Any deal above the standard approval threshold
- Any opportunity involving multiple stakeholders

Business case components:

- Problem summary
- Cost of inaction
- Desired business outcome
- Proposed solution
- Expected ROI
- Implementation requirements
- Risks and mitigations
- Decision timeline

### 6. Proposal Sent

A proposal should confirm alignment. It should not create alignment from scratch.

Before sending a proposal, confirm:

- Buyer pain
- Desired outcome
- Budget range
- Decision process
- Decision date
- Proposal audience
- Approval path
- Next meeting date

### 7. Negotiation

Negotiation should focus on scope, value, risk, and timing.

Avoid discounting unless one of the following is true:

- Scope is reduced.
- Contract term increases.
- Payment terms improve.
- Case study rights are granted.
- Strategic partner value is created.

### 8. Closed Won / Lost

Every closed opportunity requires a reason.

Closed-won notes should capture:

- Why the customer bought
- Primary value driver
- Competitor displaced
- Final pricing
- Expansion opportunity
- Delivery risks

Closed-lost notes should capture:

- Loss reason
- Competitor selected
- Pricing issue
- Timing issue
- Product gap
- No-decision reason
- Future reactivation date

### 9. Handoff / Expansion

Every closed-won deal should include a handoff to delivery or customer success.

Handoff checklist:

- Signed agreement
- Scope summary
- Buyer goals
- Success metrics
- Key stakeholders
- Risks
- Timeline
- Expansion opportunities
