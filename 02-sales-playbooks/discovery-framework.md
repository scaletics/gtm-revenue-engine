# Discovery Framework

**Owner:** Head of Sales
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Status:** Active
**Review Cadence:** Monthly
**Audience:** Sales, BD, Revenue Leadership, Client Delivery
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Purpose

Discovery determines whether a buyer has a business problem worth solving, whether the offer is relevant, and whether a real commercial opportunity exists.

The goal is not to collect information. The goal is to diagnose business impact.

## Core Discovery Structure

Use this sequence:

1. Current state
2. Pain
3. Impact
4. Desired state
5. Decision process
6. Value
7. Risks
8. Next step

## Discovery Questions

### Current State

- How are you handling this today?
- What tools, people, or processes are involved?
- What is working?
- What is not working?
- How long has this been a problem?

### Pain

- Where does the current process break down?
- What creates the most friction for your team?
- What do customers, users, or partners complain about?
- What happens when this issue is not addressed?

### Impact

- What does this cost in revenue, time, margin, churn, or missed opportunities?
- How does this affect growth?
- How does this affect customer experience?
- What internal teams are impacted?
- What happens if nothing changes in the next 90 days?

### Desired State

- What would a better operating model look like?
- What outcome would make this initiative successful?
- What would need to be true six months from now for this to be viewed as a win?
- What are you trying to improve first?

### Decision Process

- Who else is involved in this decision?
- Who owns the budget?
- Who will evaluate the solution?
- What criteria will matter most?
- What could delay the decision?

### Value

- What is the business value of solving this now?
- What metrics would improve?
- What would this allow the company to do that it cannot do today?
- How would this support revenue, margin, retention, or operational goals?

### Risks

- What concerns would your team have about moving forward?
- What has failed before?
- What would make this hard to implement?
- What internal resistance should we expect?

### Next Step

- What would be the most useful next step?
- Who needs to be included?
- What should we prepare before that conversation?
- What decision should we aim to make in the next meeting?

## Segment-Specific Discovery Guidance

### SMB SaaS

Focus on:

- Founder priorities
- Conversion
- Activation
- Churn
- Speed to revenue
- Cash efficiency
- Tool overload
- Limited team capacity

Best questions:

- What growth problem is most urgent right now?
- Where are leads or users dropping off?
- What is the fastest revenue lever if we solve this?
- What have you tried already?
- What would justify spend this month?

Avoid:

- Overly complex business-case exercises
- Enterprise-style stakeholder mapping
- Long diagnostic cycles

### Mid-Market SaaS

Focus on:

- Buying committee alignment
- ROI
- Operational efficiency
- Adoption risk
- Security and compliance
- Integration requirements
- Department-level priorities

Best questions:

- Which teams are affected by this problem?
- What makes this a priority now?
- How will the business evaluate success?
- What budget or initiative does this map to?
- Who will need to approve the final decision?
- What risk would prevent this from moving forward?

Avoid:

- Selling to only one champion
- Skipping procurement, security, or implementation complexity
- Sending proposals without decision-process clarity

### MSP / Managed Services

Focus on:

- Recurring revenue growth
- Service margin
- Ticket volume
- Customer retention
- Differentiation
- Vendor complexity
- Technician utilization
- Attach-rate expansion

Best questions:

- Which services are most profitable today?
- Where are margins being compressed?
- What recurring offers are you trying to grow?
- Which customer problems create the most support load?
- Where are you losing deals to larger MSPs or cheaper competitors?
- What would improve customer lifetime value?

Avoid:

- Positioning around tools only
- Ignoring service packaging
- Treating MSPs like traditional SaaS buyers
