**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Purpose

Closing is the process of helping the buyer make a clear decision.

The close should not rely on pressure. It should rely on alignment around problem, impact, value, timing, decision process, risk, and next step.

## Closing Readiness Checklist

Before asking for a decision, confirm:

- The buyer agrees there is a meaningful problem.
- The problem has measurable business impact.
- The proposed solution is tied to a desired outcome.
- The decision process is known.
- The decision stakeholders are identified.
- Budget has been discussed.
- Timing is clear.
- Risks have been addressed.
- Next step is scheduled.

## Closing Questions

Use these to test readiness:

- Based on what we have covered, does this solve the right problem?
- Is the business case strong enough to move forward?
- What concerns would prevent this from being approved?
- Who else needs to be comfortable before a decision is made?
- What needs to happen between now and signature?
- If we agree on scope and timing, is there a reason we could not move forward?

## Segment-Specific Closing Strategy

### SMB SaaS

Best close:

```text
Simple decision, short timeline, clear outcome.
```

Use when:

- Buyer is founder or functional lead.
- Budget is discretionary.
- Pain is urgent.
- Deal size is modest.

Close example:

```text
Based on what we discussed, the priority is improving conversion without adding more complexity. The proposed sprint gives us a focused way to fix that in 30 days. If the scope and price work, should we start this month?
```

### Mid-Market SaaS

Best close:

```text
Mutual action plan with business case and stakeholder alignment.
```

Use when:

- Multiple stakeholders are involved.
- Procurement is likely.
- Security or implementation review is needed.
- ROI must be documented.

Close example:

```text
It sounds like the business case is clear, but we still need alignment from operations, finance, and the technical evaluator. I recommend we use a mutual action plan to map the remaining steps, owners, and decision date. If we can align those stakeholders, is the team prepared to move forward?
```

### MSP / Managed Services

Best close:

```text
Commercial model tied to recurring revenue, margin, and customer retention.
```

Use when:

- Buyer cares about service profitability.
- New offer packaging is involved.
- Recurring revenue expansion is the goal.
- Owner or GM needs confidence in monetization.

Close example:

```text
The core opportunity is not just implementing the solution. It is packaging it into a recurring offer your customers will pay for. If we can define the offer, pricing, sales motion, and first customer campaign, this becomes a revenue initiative instead of an operational project. Should we move forward with the pilot?
```

## No-Decision Prevention

Most lost deals are not lost to competitors. They are lost to no decision.

Prevent no decision by confirming:

- The cost of inaction is clear.
- The buyer has a timeline.
- The buyer has a decision process.
- The economic buyer is involved.
- The initiative maps to a business priority.
- The next step is scheduled before every call ends.
