# Pitch Deck Framework

**Owner:** Sales Enablement
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Status:** Active
**Review Cadence:** Quarterly
**Audience:** Sales, BD, Revenue Leadership, Client Delivery
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Purpose

This framework defines the standard structure for sales pitch decks used across SaaS and MSP opportunities.

The deck should not be a company brochure. It should help the buyer understand the problem, the cost of inaction, the recommended solution, and the business case for moving forward.

## Recommended Deck Structure

### Slide 1: Title

Include:

- Client name
- Proposed solution or engagement
- Date
- Presenter
- Short outcome-oriented subtitle

Example subtitle:

```text
Building a repeatable GTM system for predictable pipeline, stronger conversion, and scalable revenue growth.
```

### Slide 2: Executive Context

Purpose:

Show that we understand the buyer's situation.

Include:

- Current business priority
- Market pressure
- Revenue or operational challenge
- Why this matters now

### Slide 3: Problem Statement

Purpose:

Frame the cost of the current state.

Include:

- Current challenge
- Business impact
- Operational friction
- Strategic risk

### Slide 4: Cost of Inaction

Purpose:

Create urgency.

Include:

- Revenue leakage
- Margin pressure
- Lost sales velocity
- Customer churn risk
- Competitive risk

### Slide 5: Recommended Approach

Purpose:

Show the path forward.

Include:

- Workstreams
- Methodology
- Timeline
- Key deliverables

### Slide 6: Solution Architecture

Purpose:

Show how the components fit together.

For SaaS:

```text
ICP -> Messaging -> Sales Process -> Enablement -> Pipeline Execution -> Reporting
```

For MSP:

```text
Service Packaging -> Sales Motion -> Customer Campaign -> Attach Rate -> Recurring Revenue
```

### Slide 7: Expected Outcomes

Purpose:

Connect the work to business value.

Include:

- Faster pipeline development
- Improved conversion
- Clearer positioning
- Better sales consistency
- Improved recurring revenue potential
- Stronger executive visibility

### Slide 8: Engagement Plan

Purpose:

Make execution concrete.

Include:

- Timeline
- Milestones
- Responsibilities
- Meeting cadence

### Slide 9: Investment

Purpose:

Clearly present pricing and commercial structure.

Include:

- Fee
- Payment terms
- Optional add-ons
- Performance component, if applicable

### Slide 10: Next Steps

Purpose:

Move to decision.

Include:

- Approval steps
- Contracting
- Kickoff date
- Required materials

## Segment-Specific Deck Adjustments

### SMB SaaS

Emphasize:

- Speed
- Simplicity
- Conversion
- Founder leverage
- Avoiding unnecessary hires

Reduce:

- Long implementation detail
- Enterprise process language
- Heavy stakeholder mapping

### Mid-Market SaaS

Emphasize:

- Buying committee alignment
- ROI
- Sales consistency
- Business case
- Risk reduction
- Forecast impact

Include:

- Stakeholder map
- Mutual action plan
- Executive summary slide

### MSP / Managed Services

Emphasize:

- Recurring revenue
- Service packaging
- Differentiation
- Attach-rate growth
- Margin improvement
- Vendor and channel leverage

Include:

- Offer packaging slide
- Customer expansion slide
- Partner or channel motion slide

## Deck Quality Standard

Every deck should be:

- Specific to the buyer
- Outcome-driven
- Commercially clear
- Visually simple
- Structured around decision-making
- Free from generic filler slides

## Pre-Send Checklist

Before sending a deck, confirm:

- Buyer context is accurate.
- Problem statement reflects discovery.
- Recommended approach maps to the buyer's priorities.
- Pricing is approved.
- Next step is explicit.
- Internal notes have been removed.
