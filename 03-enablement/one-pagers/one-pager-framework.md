## Purpose

Use this framework to create concise, buyer-facing one-pagers that explain the problem, the value proposition, the recommended solution, and the next step.

A one-pager should help a buyer quickly understand whether the conversation is relevant. It should not try to explain everything.

---

## One-Pager Structure

### 1. Header

Include:

- Company or offer name
- Buyer segment
- Short outcome-oriented headline
- Optional subtitle

Example:

```text
Turn Founder-Led Selling Into a Repeatable Revenue Motion
```

---

### 2. Problem Statement

Describe the buyer's current pain in plain business language.

The problem should be specific, not generic.

Weak:

```text
Many companies struggle with growth.
```

Stronger:

```text
Many early SaaS teams can sell when the founder is involved, but struggle to convert that motion into a repeatable process another seller can execute.
```

---

### 3. Why It Matters

Connect the problem to business impact.

Examples:

- Pipeline stalls
- Sales cycles lengthen
- Conversion drops
- Margins compress
- Customers do not understand value
- Founders or executives remain trapped in manual selling

---

### 4. Recommended Solution

Explain the solution in three to five bullets.

Each bullet should describe an action or system, not a vague benefit.

Example:

- Clarify ICP and buyer pain.
- Convert founder knowledge into sales messaging.
- Define sales stages and exit criteria.
- Build reusable discovery, follow-up, and proposal assets.
- Launch a focused pipeline or expansion campaign.

---

### 5. Expected Outcomes

List the outcomes the buyer should expect.

Keep these commercially relevant:

- Clearer positioning
- Better qualification
- Faster follow-up
- Stronger conversion
- More consistent sales conversations
- Improved recurring revenue potential

---

### 6. Who This Is For

Define the best-fit buyer.

Include:

- Business model
- Company stage
- Buyer role
- Trigger event
- Urgent problem

---

### 7. Engagement Snapshot

Use a short section to describe delivery.

Include:

- Timeline
- Workstreams
- Deliverables
- Meeting cadence
- Required inputs

---

### 8. Call to Action

End with one clear next step.

Examples:

```text
Schedule a 30-minute Revenue Readiness Review.
```

```text
Run a 20-minute fit assessment to identify the highest-leverage revenue bottleneck.
```

---

## Design Rules

A one-pager should be:

- One page only
- Skimmable
- Segment-specific
- Outcome-driven
- Low jargon
- Sales-ready
- Easy to convert into a slide or PDF

---

## Do Not Include

Avoid:

- Long company history
- Generic capabilities lists
- Heavy methodology language
- Unsupported ROI claims
- Too many calls to action
- Internal notes
- Confidential customer examples

---
