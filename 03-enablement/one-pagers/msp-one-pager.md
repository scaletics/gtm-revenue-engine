## Headline

Turn technical services into packaged recurring revenue offers customers understand and buy.

---

## The Problem

Many MSPs have strong technical capability, but their services are still difficult for customers to understand, compare, and buy.

The result is commoditization, weak attach rates, margin pressure, inconsistent sales conversations, and too much reliance on custom project work instead of scalable recurring revenue.

---

## Why It Matters

When service packaging and sales execution are weak:

- Customers compare the MSP against cheaper providers.
- Account managers struggle to expand existing customers.
- Technicians stay overloaded with low-margin work.
- New services fail to convert into recurring revenue.
- Vendor partnerships do not produce enough pipeline.
- Enterprise value is constrained by inconsistent recurring revenue growth.

---

## Recommended Solution

Build a managed-services commercialization system that connects service packaging, pricing logic, sales messaging, customer expansion, and partner/channel execution.

Core workstreams:

- Identify the highest-potential recurring service offer.
- Package technical capability into a customer-facing offer.
- Define pricing, value drivers, and attach-rate logic.
- Build sales talk tracks and objection handling.
- Create a customer expansion campaign.
- Support partner or vendor-led GTM activation.
- Establish a pipeline and performance review cadence.

---

## Expected Outcomes

- Clearer managed-services packaging
- Stronger customer expansion motion
- Higher attach-rate potential
- Better account manager talk tracks
- Improved service-margin focus
- More consistent sales execution
- Stronger differentiation from commodity MSPs
- Clearer path to recurring revenue growth

---

## Best Fit

This is best for MSPs and technology services companies that are:

- $1M to $50M in revenue
- Selling recurring managed services
- Launching AI, cybersecurity, cloud, Microsoft, compliance, or infrastructure services
- Trying to increase customer expansion
- Facing margin pressure
- Seeing low attach rates
- Looking to monetize vendor partnerships
- Moving away from custom project dependency

Typical deliverables:
