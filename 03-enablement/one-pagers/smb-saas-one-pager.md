## Headline

Turn founder-led selling into a repeatable revenue motion.

---

## The Problem

Many early-stage SaaS companies can sell when the founder is involved, but struggle to turn that founder knowledge into a repeatable process another person can execute.

The result is inconsistent pipeline, unclear messaging, weak follow-up, uneven demos, and difficulty knowing whether the company needs more leads, better conversion, or a clearer offer.

---

## Why It Matters

When the sales motion is not repeatable:

- The founder remains the bottleneck.
- New sales hires ramp slowly.
- Marketing produces interest that does not convert.
- Buyers receive inconsistent messages.
- Revenue depends too heavily on founder intuition.
- The company risks hiring or spending before the GTM motion is proven.

---

## Recommended Solution

Build a focused GTM foundation that documents what already works and turns it into a practical revenue system.

Core workstreams:

- Clarify ICP and best-fit buyer.
- Define the core buyer pain and value proposition.
- Convert founder sales knowledge into repeatable messaging.
- Document sales stages, discovery questions, and follow-up process.
- Build deployable outbound, one-pager, and proposal assets.
- Launch a focused pipeline or conversion improvement motion.

---

## Expected Outcomes

- Clearer ICP
- Stronger positioning
- More consistent discovery
- Better follow-up
- Faster sales execution
- Improved conversion visibility
- Reduced founder dependency
- A practical foundation for the first sales hire

---

## Best Fit

This is best for SaaS companies that are:

- Pre-revenue to $2M ARR
- Founder-led or early sales-led
- Selling to SMB or simple mid-market buyers
- Seeing inconsistent conversion
- Preparing for a first sales hire
- Trying to move beyond referrals
- Under pressure to prove repeatable revenue
