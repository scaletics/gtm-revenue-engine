# Proposal Structure

**Owner:** Revenue Operations
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Status:** Active
**Review Cadence:** Quarterly
**Audience:** Sales, Revenue Leadership, Client Delivery
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Purpose

This proposal structure is used to create clear, outcome-driven proposals for SaaS and MSP buyers.

A proposal should confirm alignment. It should not create the sale from scratch.

## Standard Proposal Sections

### 1. Executive Summary

Summarize the buyer's problem, desired outcome, and proposed path forward.

Template:

```text
[Client Name] is seeking to address [core business problem]. Based on our discussions, the primary opportunity is to [desired business outcome].

We recommend [proposed engagement / solution] to help [client] achieve [measurable outcome] within [timeline].

This proposal outlines the scope, deliverables, timeline, investment, success criteria, and next steps.
```

### 2. Current State

Include:

- Current business challenge
- Existing process or workaround
- Known constraints
- Impact of the problem
- Why now

Example:

```text
The current GTM motion relies heavily on founder-led selling and inconsistent follow-up. This creates limited pipeline visibility, uneven buyer conversations, and difficulty converting interest into repeatable revenue.
```

### 3. Desired Future State

Include:

- Target outcome
- Business metric improvement
- Operating model improvement
- Strategic value

Example:

```text
The desired future state is a repeatable sales motion with clear ICP definition, consistent messaging, documented sales stages, deployable enablement assets, and a measurable path from qualified opportunity to closed revenue.
```

### 4. Recommended Solution

Include:

- Engagement model
- Core workstreams
- Deliverables
- Timeline
- Required inputs from client

Recommended workstreams:

1. GTM Strategy
2. Sales Process
3. Sales Enablement
4. Execution Support
5. Reporting and Optimization

### 5. Deliverables

Every deliverable should define:

- What will be produced
- Format
- Owner
- Acceptance criteria
- Due date

Example:

```text
Deliverable: GTM Messaging Framework
Format: Markdown document and client-ready slide summary
Owner: GTM Lead
Acceptance Criteria: ICP, pain points, value propositions, positioning statement, and segment-specific messaging are documented and approved.
```

### 6. Success Criteria

Success criteria should be measurable.

Examples:

- ICP and segment strategy approved
- Sales process documented and implemented
- Outreach sequence deployed
- Proposal template approved
- First campaign launched
- Pipeline review cadence established
- Partner or customer targets identified

### 7. Timeline

Use a phased structure:

- Phase 1: Discovery, audit, ICP, positioning
- Phase 2: Sales process, messaging, enablement
- Phase 3: Campaign launch, proposal support, iteration
- Phase 4: Optimization, reporting, handoff

### 8. Investment

Include:

- One-time fee
- Monthly retainer, if applicable
- Performance component, if applicable
- Payment terms
- Exclusions

Pricing language:

```text
The investment for this engagement is [amount], payable as [payment structure]. This includes the deliverables listed above and does not include paid media, third-party software, data enrichment, or implementation work outside the stated scope unless otherwise agreed.
```

### 9. Assumptions and Dependencies

Examples:

- Client provides access to relevant GTM materials.
- Client identifies a primary point of contact.
- Client participates in weekly working sessions.
- Feedback is provided within agreed review windows.
- Required tools and systems are available.

### 10. Risks and Mitigations

Common risks:

- Delayed feedback
- Unclear ICP
- Weak sales adoption
- Scope creep
- Missing data
- Decision-maker misalignment

Mitigation standard:

- Set weekly checkpoints.
- Confirm document owners.
- Define acceptance criteria.
- Use change control for scope changes.
- Identify decision stakeholders early.

### 11. Next Steps

Template:

```text
To move forward:

1. Confirm scope and investment.
2. Approve timeline.
3. Execute agreement.
4. Schedule kickoff.
5. Provide access to required materials.
```

## Proposal Rule

Do not send a proposal unless the buyer has confirmed:

- Problem
- Desired outcome
- Decision process
- Budget posture
- Timeline
- Next meeting
