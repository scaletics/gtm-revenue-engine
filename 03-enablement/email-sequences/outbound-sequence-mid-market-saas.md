# Outbound Sequence: Mid-Market SaaS

**Owner:** Sales Enablement
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Review Cadence:** Monthly
**Status:** Active
**Audience:** Sales, BD
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

---

## Audience

CROs, VPs of Sales, VPs of Marketing, RevOps leaders, CEOs, and business unit leaders at mid-market SaaS companies.

---

## Primary Use Case

Use this sequence when targeting mid-market SaaS companies with stalled pipeline, inconsistent messaging, sales-process drift, poor conversion, long sales cycles, or weak business-case development.

---

## Sequence Logic

| Step | Channel | Timing |
|---:|---|---:|

| 2 | LinkedIn view / connect | Day 2 |

| 4 | Call | Day 5 |

## Email 1: Pipeline Conversion Problem

Subject:

```text
Question on stalled SaaS pipeline
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

Many mid-market SaaS teams have pipeline, but too much of it stalls between discovery, proposal, business case, and decision.

The issue is often not demand alone. It is inconsistent qualification, unclear value messaging, weak stakeholder alignment, or sales process drift across the team.

Curious, is {{Company}} focused more on creating new pipeline right now, or improving conversion and velocity inside the existing funnel?

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Email 2: Revenue Infrastructure Insight

Subject:

```text
Where SaaS revenue teams usually lose velocity
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

One pattern I see with mid-market SaaS companies is that revenue teams often have the right people and tools, but the operating system between them is inconsistent.

That usually shows up as:

- Different reps qualifying differently.
- Messaging changing by seller.
- Champions lacking a strong internal business case.
- Proposals going out before decision process is clear.
- Forecasts depending more on rep judgment than stage evidence.

If useful, I can share a quick framework for identifying where sales velocity is breaking down.

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Email 3: Use Case

Subject:

```text
Potential fit for {{Company}}
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

For SaaS teams like {{Company}}, the opportunity is often to tighten the revenue infrastructure between ICP, messaging, sales stages, enablement, business case, and proposal process.

When those pieces are aligned, teams usually get cleaner qualification, stronger stakeholder conversations, fewer stalled deals, and more consistent forecast visibility.

Would it be worth a short conversation to see whether there is a sales-process or conversion issue worth solving?

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Email 4: Breakup / Trigger

Subject:

```text
Should I close this out?
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

I have not heard back, so I will close this out for now.

The reason I reached out is that many mid-market SaaS teams do not need more activity as much as they need better conversion discipline across qualification, messaging, stakeholder alignment, and business-case development.

If pipeline velocity, forecast quality, or sales-process consistency becomes a priority at {{Company}}, happy to reconnect.

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Usage Notes

Customize by replacing:

- Target department
- Sales-process pain
- Funnel stage issue
- Current trigger event
- Competitive pressure
- Proof point, when available

Keep the email executive-level. Avoid tactical tool language unless the buyer has already raised tooling as a problem.
