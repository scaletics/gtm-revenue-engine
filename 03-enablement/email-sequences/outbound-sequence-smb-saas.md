# Outbound Sequence: SMB SaaS

**Owner:** Sales Enablement
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Review Cadence:** Monthly
**Status:** Active
**Audience:** Sales, BD
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

---

## Audience

Founders, CEOs, heads of growth, and first sales hires at early-stage SaaS companies.

---

## Primary Use Case

Use this sequence when targeting SMB SaaS companies that need to move beyond founder-led selling, clarify positioning, improve conversion, or build a repeatable sales motion.

---

## Sequence Logic

| Step | Channel | Timing |
|---:|---|---:|
| 1 | Email | Day 1 |
| 2 | LinkedIn view / connect | Day 2 |
| 3 | Email | Day 4 |
| 4 | Call | Day 5 |
| 5 | Email | Day 8 |
| 6 | LinkedIn message | Day 10 |
| 7 | Email | Day 14 |

---

## Email 1: Founder-Led Sales Problem

Subject:

```text
Quick question on founder-led sales
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

A lot of early-stage SaaS teams reach a point where the founder can sell the product, but the sales motion is not repeatable yet.

Usually the issue is not effort. It is unclear ICP, inconsistent messaging, weak follow-up, or a sales process that still lives in the founder's head.

Curious, is {{Company}} still mostly founder-led on sales, or have you started building a repeatable motion?

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Email 2: Conversion Insight

Subject:

```text
Where early SaaS teams usually leak revenue
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

One pattern I see with early SaaS companies is that pipeline generation often gets blamed when the real issue is conversion.

The common breakdowns are:

- ICP is too broad.
- Buyers do not understand the pain clearly.
- Demos are not tied to business outcomes.
- Follow-up is inconsistent.
- Pricing and packaging create friction.

If useful, I can share a simple checklist for spotting where the sales motion is leaking revenue.

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Email 3: Use Case

Subject:

```text
Potential fit for {{Company}}
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

For a SaaS company like {{Company}}, the fastest path to revenue may not be adding more tools or hiring too early.

It may be documenting the sales motion that already works when the founder sells, then turning it into repeatable messaging, discovery, follow-up, and proposal assets.

That gives the next sales hire or growth motion a clearer system to execute.

Would it be worth a short conversation to see whether there is a repeatable revenue motion to build from what is already working?

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Email 4: Breakup / Trigger

Subject:

```text
Should I close the loop?
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

I have not heard back, so I will close the loop for now.

The reason I reached out is that many SaaS founders are close to repeatability, but their positioning, sales process, and follow-up are not yet documented well enough for another person to execute.

If improving conversion or moving beyond founder-led selling becomes a priority for {{Company}}, happy to reconnect.

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

---

## Usage Notes

Customize by replacing:

- Founder-led sales angle
- Primary conversion issue
- Product category
- Buyer trigger
- Proof point, when available

Do not overcomplicate the sequence. SMB SaaS buyers need speed, relevance, and a simple next step.
