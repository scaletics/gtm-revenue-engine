# Outbound Sequence: MSP

**Owner:** Sales Enablement
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Status:** Active
**Review Cadence:** Monthly
**Audience:** Sales, BD
**Client Reuse:** Client-Customizable

## Audience

MSP owners, CEOs, sales leaders, and service leaders.

## Primary Use Case

Use this sequence when targeting MSPs that may need help packaging, selling, or scaling AI, cybersecurity, cloud, Microsoft, compliance, or managed services into recurring revenue.

## Sequence Logic

| Step | Channel | Timing |
|---:|---|---:|
| 1 | Email | Day 1 |
| 2 | LinkedIn view / connect | Day 2 |
| 3 | Email | Day 4 |
| 4 | Call | Day 5 |
| 5 | Email | Day 8 |
| 6 | LinkedIn message | Day 10 |
| 7 | Email | Day 14 |

## Email 1: Problem Relevance

Subject:

```text
Quick question on recurring services
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

Many MSPs are being pushed to offer AI, cybersecurity, and cloud services, but the harder part is turning those capabilities into recurring revenue customers understand and buy.

Curious, is {{Company}} currently focused on packaging any new managed services this year, or mostly expanding existing accounts?

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

## Email 2: Commercial Insight

Subject:

```text
Where MSPs usually get stuck
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

One pattern I see with MSPs is that the technical capability usually exists before the commercial packaging does.

The issue is rarely, "Can we deliver this?"

It is usually:

- Can sales explain it clearly?
- Can customers understand the value?
- Can it attach to existing accounts?
- Can it become recurring revenue instead of another project?

If useful, I can share a simple framework for evaluating which services are most ready to commercialize.

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

## Email 3: Use Case

Subject:

```text
Potential fit for {{Company}}
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

For MSPs like {{Company}}, the biggest opportunity is often inside the existing customer base.

The goal is to identify which services can be packaged into a clearer recurring offer, then give the sales team the talk track, campaign, pricing logic, and objection handling to drive attach rate.

This is especially relevant for AI, Microsoft, cybersecurity, compliance, cloud, and managed infrastructure services.

Would it be worth a short conversation to see whether there is a revenue opportunity inside your current customer base?

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

## Email 4: Breakup / Trigger

Subject:

```text
Should I close the loop?
```

Body:

```text
Hi {{First Name}},

I have not heard back, so I will close the loop for now.

The reason I reached out is that many MSPs are under pressure to grow recurring revenue, but their newer services are not always packaged in a way customers can easily understand or buy.

If AI, cybersecurity, cloud, or Microsoft service commercialization becomes a priority for {{Company}}, happy to reconnect.

Best,
{{Sender Name}}
```

## Usage Notes

Customize by replacing:

- Service category
- Buyer trigger
- Local market context
- Existing customer expansion angle
- Proof point, when available

Do not over-personalize the first email. Keep it short and relevant.
