## Purpose

Use this checklist to determine whether an MSP opportunity is worth pursuing.

This checklist is designed for managed service providers, cybersecurity service providers, Microsoft partners, cloud partners, hosting firms, and technology services companies that need to package or expand recurring revenue services.

## Must-Have Criteria

Do not pursue unless at least three are true:

- They have recurring revenue services.
- They want to launch or improve a higher-value offer.
- They have an existing customer base.
- They have owner or executive involvement.
- They can implement within 90 days.
- They understand the need for packaging, not just technical delivery.

## Discovery Questions

### Revenue Model

- What percentage of revenue is recurring today?
- Which services are most profitable?
- Which services are hardest to sell?
- Which services create the most support load?

### Packaging

- Which services are currently standardized?
- Which services are still sold as custom projects?
- Where do customers struggle to understand the value?
- What offers need clearer pricing or packaging?

### Sales Capacity

- Who owns new logo sales?
- Who owns account expansion?
- Does the team have sales talk tracks?
- Are account managers trained to sell higher-value services?

### Customer Base

- How many active customers exist today?
- Which customers are best-fit for expansion?
- Which services could be attached to existing accounts?
- What customer segment has the strongest willingness to pay?

## Disqualification Criteria

Disqualify if:

- They only want free strategy.
- They have no customer base.
- They have no sales capacity.
- They refuse to standardize offers.
- They want custom delivery for every customer.
- They do not believe customers will pay for higher-value services.
