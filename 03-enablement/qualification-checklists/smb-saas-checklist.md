## Purpose

Use this checklist to determine whether an SMB SaaS opportunity is worth pursuing.

This checklist is designed for founder-led, early-stage, or lean SaaS companies that need to clarify positioning, improve conversion, build pipeline, or create a repeatable sales motion before hiring too aggressively.

---

## Best-Fit Profile

A strong SMB SaaS opportunity usually has:

- Founder-led selling or first sales hire
- Pre-revenue to $2M ARR
- Simple buying process
- Short sales cycle
- Clear revenue urgency
- Unclear ICP or messaging
- Inconsistent pipeline or conversion
- Willingness to execute quickly

## Must-Have Criteria

Do not pursue unless at least three are true:

- Founder or CEO is directly involved.
- There is a real revenue problem.
- The company has a product that can be sold now.
- The buyer has budget or can approve discretionary spend.
- The team can implement within 30 to 60 days.
- There is a clear path to pipeline, conversion, or activation improvement.

---

## Discovery Questions

### Revenue Urgency

- What revenue target are you trying to hit in the next 90 days?
- What is currently blocking growth?
- What happens if this does not improve this quarter?
- Is the priority more pipeline, better conversion, or clearer positioning?

### Founder Commitment

- Who is currently doing most of the selling?
- How much founder time is going into sales each week?
- What parts of the sales motion are still trapped in the founder's head?
- What would need to be documented before another person could sell this effectively?

### ICP and Messaging

- Who is the highest-converting customer today?
- Which customers are easiest to close?
- Which customers create the most friction?
- What language do buyers use to describe the problem?
- What is the clearest reason a customer should buy now?

### Offer Readiness

- Is the product ready to sell now?
- Is pricing clear?
- Is the onboarding process clear?
- What must be true for a customer to get value quickly?
- Are there any product gaps that prevent sales?

### Conversion

- Where are prospects dropping off?
- Are demos converting?
- Are proposals converting?
- Are trials converting?
- Are buyers stalling after the first call?

---

## Disqualification Criteria

Disqualify or nurture if:

- The product is not ready to sell.
- The founder is not involved.
- The company wants strategy but will not execute.
- There is no budget.
- The problem is not tied to revenue.
- The team is still searching for the market.
- The buyer only wants free advice.

---

## Recommended Next Step by Score

### 32-40: Pursue Immediately

Recommend a focused GTM sprint, positioning sprint, sales-process build, or outbound activation.

### 24-31: Pursue Selectively

Continue discovery. Confirm urgency, budget, and implementation capacity before proposal.

### 16-23: Nurture

Send educational material. Revisit when the company has more urgency, budget, or execution capacity.

### Below 16: Do Not Pursue

Do not invest sales time unless a strong trigger event appears.
