## Purpose

Use this checklist to determine whether a mid-market SaaS opportunity is worth pursuing.

This checklist is designed for B2B SaaS companies with longer sales cycles, multiple stakeholders, sales-led or hybrid GTM motions, and meaningful revenue operations complexity.

---

## Best-Fit Profile

A strong mid-market SaaS opportunity usually has:

- $2M to $50M ARR
- Sales-led or hybrid GTM motion
- 3 to 6 month sales cycle
- Multiple stakeholders
- Pipeline conversion issues
- Inconsistent sales process
- Executive revenue priority
- Need for business case, enablement, or sales operating discipline

## Must-Have Criteria

Do not pursue unless at least four are true:

- Executive sponsor is involved.
- The problem is tied to revenue, retention, conversion, or sales velocity.
- There is access to sales-process or pipeline data.
- Multiple stakeholders can be engaged.
- Budget exists or can be created.
- The company can implement changes within 60 to 90 days.
- There is clear opportunity for expansion or follow-on work.

---

## Discovery Questions

### Revenue Impact

- Where is revenue performance currently below target?
- Which stage of the funnel is underperforming?
- Are deals stalling, slipping, or getting lost to no decision?
- What would improve if this problem were solved in the next two quarters?

### Sales Process

- What sales stages exist today?
- Are exit criteria clearly defined?
- Where do reps interpret the process differently?
- How accurate is the forecast?
- What does the team struggle to document consistently?

### Stakeholders

- Who owns the revenue problem?
- Who owns the budget?
- Who must approve process changes?
- Who will use the assets or process day to day?
- Who could block the initiative?

### Business Case

- What business priority does this initiative support?
- What metric must improve?
- What is the cost of inaction?
- What would justify the investment internally?
- What risks must be reduced before approval?

### Implementation

- Who will own adoption internally?
- What systems are involved?
- What enablement is required?
- What change-management risk exists?
- What timeline is realistic?

---

## Disqualification Criteria

Disqualify or nurture if:

- No executive sponsor exists.
- The problem is not tied to a business metric.
- Stakeholders cannot be accessed.
- The company will not share enough GTM context.
- Internal politics block execution.
- Procurement burden exceeds deal value.
- The initiative is exploratory with no owner.

---

## Recommended Next Step by Score

### 32-40: Pursue Immediately

Recommend a GTM diagnostic, revenue infrastructure sprint, sales-process redesign, or enablement build.

### 24-31: Pursue Selectively

Continue discovery. Confirm executive sponsorship, business case, and implementation capacity before proposal.

### 16-23: Nurture

Send executive education or benchmarking material. Revisit when urgency increases.

### Below 16: Do Not Pursue

Avoid heavy sales investment unless a senior stakeholder creates a new trigger event.
